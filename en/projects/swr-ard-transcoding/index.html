Central ARD transcoding: taking over mid-project, go-live included

Context

Germany's ARD public broadcasters worked separately for a long time, including on technical infrastructure. Under cost pressure, they began to cooperate more closely. A central transcoding system was to bring the different camera types and file formats of the various broadcasters into one shared playout system, for both on-demand content and live streams.

Starting point

I joined as a second project lead to support the existing one. The concept was already in place and implementation was underway. Then my colleague was given another assignment within the company, and I took over the project completely, right in the critical phase before go-live.

My role

First supporting project lead, then fully responsible project lead including go-live. Afterwards, I supported the search for a new project lead and onboarded and handed over to the new external project lead.

Approach

The most critical point was live: with on-demand, a mistake can be corrected, with a live broadcast it cannot. For this, I developed a high-availability concept and had it tested. In addition, an SLA with different service levels and 24-hour support, split across two support departments with different capability profiles, one with extended capabilities until 5 pm, one for night operations. I prepared the handover to the new project lead in a structured way so that no knowledge would be lost.

Outcome

The go-live was successful, and the system ran stably afterwards without major outages. The handover to the new external project lead worked without any break in the project.

What I take away

You rarely take over projects at the beginning. The value lies in getting up to speed quickly in a running system, carrying responsibility when it counts, and handing it over cleanly once the right person has been found.
