The Infinite Playa: Burning Man as a virtual world

Context

When the Burning Man festival could not take place physically, The Infinite Playa was created: one of the officially recognized virtual worlds of the Burning Man "Virtual Burn". A photorealistic, freely explorable recreation of the playa of Black Rock City, accessible via browser on desktop and mobile as well as via VR headset. Users created an avatar, moved freely through the desert, visited art installations, stages and social spaces, and experienced live performances. The project's goal went beyond a single event: connecting live events with persistent virtual spaces.

Starting point

Adastra Studios was one of the studios behind the platform. The core team comprised over 30 permanent employees from various countries, plus outsourcing partners in Ukraine, Croatia, Poland and the USA. Many teams, many time zones, many interfaces, and a deadline that could not move: a festival does not wait.

My role

I joined the project in a support role when a lot was already underway. Over the course of the project, I grew into the lead, not through a formal title but because at certain interfaces nobody else was doing the translation between the teams. In the end, I was independently responsible for the part of the project contributed by Adastra.

Approach

My main job was coordination under pressure: keeping distributed teams across several countries and time zones in sync, managing dependencies between internal developers and outsourcing partners, and making sure the work streams fit together when they come together. On top of that came the translation between disciplines, from 3D art to development to the requirements of the event side.

The last month before the event became especially challenging. This phase was actually reserved for testing and bug fixing, yet stakeholders kept coming with new change requests. Here I had to communicate clearly: major changes this close to the deadline put the entire project at risk. So we drew a clear line and only implemented smaller adjustments that would not endanger the project, and even those only if enough time remained and everything ran stably. With a festival on a fixed date, there is no second attempt.

Outcome

The live events ran successfully. The platform existed beyond the project's duration and served further clients.

What I take away

In distributed teams, leadership emerges at the interfaces. Whoever closes the gaps between teams takes on responsibility long before it appears on a business card. This project showed me that this is exactly my strength.
