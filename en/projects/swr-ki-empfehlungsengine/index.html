AI recommendation system in production · Product Owner · SWR/ARD, in collaboration with ZDF · 2023 to 2025

Personalized recommendations for the SWR website

Context

The SWR website covers editorial content across a wide range of topics, from news to sports to science. Below every article, readers see recommendations for further reading. These recommendations play a big part in whether readers stay on the site or leave.

Starting point

Until then, recommendations were largely curated by editors by hand or came from a legacy system. Neither approach scaled with the publishing volume, and both tied up editorial capacity that was needed elsewhere.

My role

As Product Owner, I was responsible for the product in a Scrum setup. The team included a Scrum Master, data scientists, backend developers and a data analyst. On top of that came requirements planning and alignment in a joint coordination group with ZDF, Germany's second major public broadcaster.

Approach

We built a recommendation system based on a machine learning model that was continuously tested, trained and fine-tuned. The model took into account publication time, topic, semantic metadata and categories, among other factors.

For fine-tuning, we needed editorial feedback. The real problem was not the technology but the editors' time: they were fully booked and simply had no room for another project. So we made the evaluation tool radically simple. Two recommendation variants side by side, one click on the better one, done. A few seconds per rating, no onboarding needed. Only that way did enough feedback come in, and exactly this feedback flowed back into the model as a training signal.

Outcome

After go-live, we compared views and clicks on the recommendations with the historical numbers from the legacy system. The new system performed measurably better and ran in production permanently.

What I take away

AI projects in editorial environments rarely fail because of the technology. They fail because the people whose knowledge you need have no time. If you make giving feedback as easy as possible, you actually get it.
