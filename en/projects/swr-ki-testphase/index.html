AI pilot phase at SWR: from experiment to permanent structure

Context

Like many media companies, SWR faced the question of how to integrate AI tools into daily work in a meaningful way. A cross-departmental AI group was set up to test and evaluate new tools in a structured way.

Starting point

The market for AI tools was crowded and growing fast. Every department had its own needs, but nobody had an overview of which tools actually delivered value and which just sounded good. What was missing was a shared methodology for evaluating them.

My role

At the time, I was already deeply into AI in my personal life and regularly shared what I learned with my managers. That is how the additional role came to me: I represented product development in the cross-departmental AI group, brought in use cases from the department and helped shape the evaluation methodology. In parallel, I was the go-to person for AI topics within the department.

Approach

We tested various tools against requirements we had collected from the departments beforehand. Evaluation followed clear criteria: Does the tool deliver real value? Does the work get better? Does it save time? Does the result improve? Can it save money? From my department, I brought concrete use cases, especially the question of what could flow into existing products.

Outcome

Several AI tools found a permanent place in the company through this process. The pilot phase turned into a permanent AI structure that continued beyond my contract.

What I take away

The difference between playing with AI and getting value from AI lies in the evaluation methodology. If you define what "better" concretely means before testing, you end up making decisions instead of following gut feelings.
